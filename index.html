EPiQS Moore Workshop

Bridging Quantum Materials and Quantum Information

September 14–16, 2026 · North Carolina State University · Raleigh, NC

Meeting Goals

Supported by the program managers of the EPiQS (Emergent Phenomena in Quantum Systems) initiative of the Gordon and Betty Moore Foundation, this three-day workshop is designed to bridge the quantum information and quantum materials communities. We believe the two communities can greatly benefit from one another, and we are convening a new kind of community whose mission is twofold:

  • to advance the understanding of quantum materials by incorporating insights from other disciplines; and
  • to highlight and promote the fundamental importance of basic science for quantum materials, quantum information, and other quantum disciplines.

The program is intentionally unconventional. Two framing talks from the quantum materials community will identify current challenges, and sixteen speakers from the quantum information community will explore how their tools and insights can help address them. The audience is drawn chiefly from the quantum condensed matter community. At the close of the workshop, participants will draft a short vision document identifying opportunities for future collaboration and knowledge exchange between the two fields.

Focus Topics

Quantum Measurement Beyond Linear Response
Spins, Defects, and Solid-State Quantum Sensors
Quantum Computing: Hardware, Architectures, and Algorithms
Entanglement, Simulation, and Emergent Phases
